Q: Are you a different person because of what you experienced?

A: Yes. When I was still with my parents, even in the ghetto, I was not only — I was an only child. I was — trying to think of the right word, I was doted upon. My mother would feed me — first the nan — my nanny and then my mother would feed me til I was probably about seven or eight years old, because I didn’t like to eat. And so there was always somebody taking care of me, I was really well taken care of. And because I was with my family, and with — with my parents, I didn’t feel that I had to behave in any certain way. I don’t know how I did behave, but I’m sure I could do whatever I really wanted, if 1t was safe. But when I left the ghetto, and I was with strangers, I learned to be not only obedient, but not to annoy other people. And so I think that I became more tolerant of others.
